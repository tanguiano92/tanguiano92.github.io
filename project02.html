Title: Lost Soul


Medium: Digital Video/Video Collage


Duration: 1 min.


Attributes:
"Braan na Beatha" composed by Adrian von Zeigler;
"Hands in Fog" by Life of Pix;
"Walking Around the Forest" by Pixabay;
"A Park's View of Autumn Trees on a Foggy Day" by INNORECORDS;
"Driving Along a Foggy Road" by Asif Khan


March 11, 2019


I created this video to conjure a feeling of uneasiness in my audience. I believe unsettling
images and videos are more influential than those that are not, therefore I created "Lost Soul".
The story of the video is a wandering soul trying to find its way out of the fog, looking for
the end of the mist and the forest. Perhaps it is in purgatory, and does not know how long it has
been lost for. I wanted this short film to be open-ended and force the viewers to be left haunted. by
The impression of this film is sadness, emptiness and endlessness. These are the feelings I had
experienced during the ten years I suffered from deep chronic depression. In which I felt like a lost
soul and was not sure if I would continue on each day up until about a year and a half ago when I over-
came it. This small film collage loosely represents how I felt walking through life each day, faking a,
smile and trying to hide what was really going on inside.


Title: Lost in the Shadow of Infamy


Medium: Digital Video/Video Collage


Duration: 3:34 min.


Attributes:
"CBS News Report on Hunt for Zodiac Killer" "Zodiac on Jim Dunbar" Images from Google Images

September 17, 2019


Toni_Anguiano_Proj01 from Toni Anguiano on Vimeo.


When I was thnking of ideas for this project, many possibilities ran through my
mind but none of them felt right to me. I could have made a cultural comment on the usual
things like social media, politics and what have you, even though I am passionate about those topics,
I wanted to deviate into something that held meaning to me. I was driving home from class one day
and drove past Lake Herman Road in Benicia, as I had many times before, but this time was different.
I began to think about the unsolved Zodiac murders and as I thought about them, I realized I knew so
much about the Zodiac but I could not remember not even one of the victims names. Not a one. And it
dawned on me: this is a cultural and media issue, and a big one at that. When tragedies happen, our media
and news broadcasters often fixate on the criminal and floss over the victims. They give us the names
of the victims but plaster the villian's face, name, and method of killing all over everything in which
the public forgets the innocent people who lost their lives and focus on the criminal. There are many serial
killer fanpages and documentaries out there that discuss serial killers and their psychology in an attempt to
understand what made them into monsters. Was it nurture vs. nature? Did they have a sexual deviancy in youth?
The media will exploit these characters as though they are celebrities and it often causes other mentally unstable
people to idolize these monsters and commit acts of violence of their own. The point of my project is to
push people to remember the victims and forget the monsters. Showing these awful people in the news and giving them
television segments and interviews is doing more harm than it is good for our society. We need to stop glorifying
these heinous acts and the unstable people who committed them and honor the innocent people who were murdered and left
in the shadow of their killers. As though they belong to that person. When you say Jeffrey Dahmer, Richard Ramirez,
Charles Manson, or John Wayne Gacy, to name a few, there is no doubt that someone won't know who they are or what
they have done. But ask those same people what any of the victims names are, there is a good chance you'll get a blank
stare in response. In most cases communities want to forget the
the tragedy and the murderer who caused them,
and with that the victims go forgotten along with their killer. Is that fair? No. Therefore I made this piece to
pull the victoms out of the Zodiac's shadow as well as expose him for the attention-seeker he truly was.
He was a coward who simply wanted attention, and took the lives of four people to get it.
